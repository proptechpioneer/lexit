Why Most Landlords are
Losing Money

New Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) standards

Upgrading to meet new EPC standards will be extremely costly — many properties will require expensive refurbishments just to comply. Worst of all, it will soon be illegal to rent out any property that doesn't meet these minimum standards.

Renters' Rights Act 2025

The new Renters Rights Act skyrockets your risks as a landlord. Increasing the rent to market value will now be far more challenging, leaving your income stagnant while costs soar.

Removing trouble tenants? An even bigger legal nightmare, with hefty penalties and expanded tenant rights to claw back rent, turning enforcement into a minefield.

Your potential returns shrink as legal pitfalls and costly mistakes grow. One wrong move could cost thousands, trap you with difficult tenants longer, and wipe out your profits. The risks outweigh the rewards—unless you're ready to face a far more vulnerable investment landscape.

Finance Act 2015

The Finance Act 2015 ended mortgage interest tax relief for landlords. That was manageable at record-low rates, but with rising interest costs, your profits are shrinking. No going back — your costs are only going up.
